OS

All of my professinal work is done on a Windows machine. At home I split my development between a Windows machine and a server running Ubuntu.

hardware

  • An uninteresting PC
    • It has a 6 year old CPU, 16Gb of RAM and an RTX 2070 super.
  • NiZ Plum84 electro-capacitive keyboard
    • I've used lots of mechanical keyboards and this is the one that gives me the least RSI problems. I use the model with 35g switches.
  • Anker Vertical Mouse
    • This did wonders for my mouse-based RSI. For Â£15 so there's no excuse not to at least try one.

terminal

nice software

This is a list of software that makes me happy.

  • Firefox
    • I switched from Chrome a few years ago and have never missed it.
  • toggl
    • Time tracking is great, everyone should know where their time is really going, as opposed to where they think it's going. Toggl also rewrote their Window desktop app to be native instead of electron based, another small victory.
  • Dynalist
    • It took me a while to warm up to Dynalist but now I keep half my life in there.
  • Everything
    • Find files quickly.
  • premake
    • Project file generator I use for C or C++. Avoid dealing with nightmare visual studio solutions or gigantic makefiles.
  • Greenshot
    • Screenshot tool that gets out of the way.

music

If you were just here for the tech, you can stop here.

guitars

  • Fender Jaguar
    • Late 90s CIJ model, AVRI pickups, rhythm circiut disconnected. I love it and will only part with it by force.
  • Guyatone Mosrite copy
    • A very odd thing from the 70s. It has two odd shaped P90 pickups and a very interesting tone circuit called a varitone.
  • Epiphone Wilshire
    • Recent reissue. Dubbed the "soapshire" since the body and all of the hardware are white.
  • Aria Diamond DM-380
    • Awesome cheap mosrite copy that sounds huge. Shame about the Strat bridge.
  • Simon & Patrick SP6 Cedar
    • Cedar top acoustic. Mine is all beaten up and I love it
  • Yamaha FG700MS
    • Bought on whim when I decided I wanted a spruce top acoustic. For the price I couldn't be happier. I would recommend these cheap Yamaha acoustics to anyone.

amps

  • Bugera V22
    • This amp is great, it sounds very neutral and works a blank canvas for effects.
  • Fender Vibro Champ
    • Silverface model from '76 or '77.
